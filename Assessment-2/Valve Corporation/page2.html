ABOUT US!

Valve Corporation is an American video game company founded in 1996 by Gabe Newell and Mike Harrington, former Microsoft employees. Valve is known for their well-known video games like Counter-Strike, Half-Life, Portal, and Team Fortress that sold millions of copies around the world, with Counter-Strike 2 (Valve's most popular game) with a monthly player count ranging from 780,000 to 963,000 in 2024.


Gabe Newell, a Harvard University dropout, started his journey working for Microsoft, where he spent the next 13 years developing early versions of the Windows operating system. Gabe Newell went on to produce Windows 1.0 and Windows 2.0 and played a key role in making Windows a gaming platform by porting Doom to Windows 95.

In 1996, Gabe Newell, after receiving lots of credit from Microsoft and earning him millions of dollars, partnered up with his co-worker Mike Harrington and started their own gaming company using their fortunes. By November 1998, they released their debut game, Half-Life, which became a commercial success with 250,000 to 300,000 copies sold by the end of the year


By September 12, 2003, Valve released a digital distribution platform known as "Steam," which would soon become one of the most popular sites for gamers to purchase and download games not just from Valve, but from many different gaming companies, too. Currently, Steam has over 105,000 games, with over 132 million users from around the world.

As of today, Valve is valued at approximately $8 billion, with a total of 623 million copies of its games sold from all platforms.
